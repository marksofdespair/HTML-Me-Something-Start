I've been around professional coders since I was first brought home, because my dad is one. I talk about him a lot because I'm proud of him and respect him as a professional, but he's also the reason I'm here. One of my earliest core memories I have is of him taking me to an office he used to work at, and introducing me to some of his coworkers. I was only around 4 years of age. They had a candy drawer for tough days, and I got to pick a piece of candy out. It was a 'sweet' introduction - but it was also when I first became interested in what he did!

Later, he moved on from working with code, and currently works as a kubernetes specialist, specializing in CI/CD pipelines and infastructure and a senior cloud engineer. He started doing things I'm doing, and moved up because he worked hard and never stopped learning, and that is the core of being a developer and engineer. It's something I aspire to, even if I don't stay in coding forever- always learning is always admirable.

He's been an indispensable resource, always happy to answer my questions and explain topics and concepts that I might be confused on, or lacking understanding. So I'd like his story to be the start of my story in my journey as a programmer. I'm truthfully not entirely sure what my trajectory is when it comes to my career, but I do know I'll have a lot of guidance in whatever I choose to do.

Myself - Past, and Hobbies

Growing up, I was always an animal lover! I've had many pets of many different species over the years, and loved and cared for all of them. When I was 6, I really wanted to be a vet. As I grew older, however, I realized- well, that's a lot of work. It wasn' that I wasn't willing to do the work, it was just VERY intimidating, and I wondered if maybe it wasn't realistic the older I got, so then I wanted to be a vet tech. However, starting in high school, I started growing more and more interested in coding. I learned about it off and on as a hobby, but never took classes or did anything with it beyond self teaching. When I graduated high school, I went to a technical school to become a vet tech. However, due to health related circumstances, I ended up dropping out of the programme.

To this day, animals and plants remain a great interest of mine. I train dogs as a hobby, and I currently care for 3 dogs and look after 32 VERY dramatic plants (8 of which are cuttings I'm in the process of propagating). Plants and animal care will always be my passion, and I think my ideal job would be something that would allow me to use my skills as a coder combined with my passion for animals, video games, or plants. Maybe all of the above, but that's wishful thinking!
